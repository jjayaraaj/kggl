Tunnelling

KGGL is widely supplying “Grouting material” worldwide. KGGL Grout plays an important role in improving the ground stability by way of increased strength and reducing permeability of treated soils. KGGL Binder can effectively be used for:

  • Logging operation
  • Support of footing
  • Tunnel Support
  • Pit excavation
  • Grouted cut-off wall and
  • Pipeline supports

KGGL Binder soluble silicate has been used successfully in chemical grouting and other geotechnical applications for more than 30 years. For Soil Stabilization and water control during excavation and tunneling projects, this grout offers several advantages

  • Reliable and Proven performance
  • Safety and ease of use
  • Environmental acceptability
  • Adaptability over a wide range of applications

Suitable silicates are generally considered to be among the strongest and least toxic of the existing chemical grouts. Soluble silicates are inorganic, amorphous glasses.

KGGL grout is a low-viscosity, chemical grout system used for ground consolidation and stabilization of foundation and footings located on fine to medium sands. Other important uses include control of water inflow into shafts and tunnels during excavation, pre-grouting of TBM/ EPBM tunnel headings, formation of impervious grout curtains and cut-off walls at dam sites;

KGGL grouts are best suited for treating fine sand with mean particle size ranging from 1 to 0.1mm, which cannot be handled by cement grout.

The physicochemical and mechanical properties of KGGL Grout depends on the characteristics of grout permeancy, Gel-Time control, Temperature sensitivity, Toxicity, Low initial viscosity, water solubility, and economic factor in addition to Strength.

Engineering criteria to be used for Grout Evaluation are viscosity, setting time, Penetrability, strength of set grout and grouted mass, durability, and toxicity

Steel

KGGL manufactures the Effective Binder for Steel Industry, KGGL formulated the best bonding agent used for briquetting and pelletizing, KGGL can confidently say our proprietary bonding agent has gone through various in-house testing and has been perfected:

The Salient features of KGGL Binder:

KGGL Binder plays a crucial role in the successful production of DRI briquettes by acting as a binder, reducing agent, fluxing agent, and temperature regulator. It helps optimize the DRI production process, making it more efficient and cost-effective

KGGL Binder: holding the iron ore particles together in the briquettes. This helps maintain the briquettes' structural integrity, preventing them from falling apart during handling, transportation, and the DRI production process.

Reducing Agent: KGGL Binder contains silica (SiO2), which can act as a reducing agent in the DRI process. During the reduction of iron ore to produce metallic iron, it can help in the removal of oxygen from the iron ore, facilitating the conversion of iron ore into DRI.

Fluxing Agent KGGL Binder can also function as a fluxing agent. It can modify the composition and viscosity of the slag produced during the DRI reduction process. This can be beneficial for controlling the slag's properties and ensuring it doesn't hinder the reduction reaction.

Temperature Control KGGL Binder can contribute to temperature control within the briquettes during the reduction process. It helps regulate the heat distribution, ensuring that the reduction reaction occurs efficiently without overheating or underheating the briquettes.
